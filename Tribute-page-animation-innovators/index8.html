A Little Disney Background

Walt Disney was born in 1901 in Chicago, Illinois. He had four siblings. From a very early age, he loved to draw and create silly characters born from his imagination. His older brother, Roy, was an early admirer and encouraged him to pursue his dream as a cartoonist. While Walt was in high school, he regularly canvased his neighborhood, selling his cartoons to anyone who would buy them. When he was 18, he joined the Red Cross Ambulance Corps and was stationed in France for one year. In addition to medical supplies and equipment, it is rumored his ambulance was plastered with cartoon drawings everywhere he went!

Disney Movie Classics

Walt Disney was an American animator, film producer and entrepreneur. A pioneer of the American animation industry, he introduced several developments in the production of cartoons. As a film producer, he holds the record for most Academy Awards earned and nominations by an individual, having won 22 Oscars from 59 nominations. Bambi is a 1942 American animated drama film directed by David Hand (supervising a team of sequence directors), produced by Walt Disney and based on the 1923 book Bambi, a Life in the Woods by Austrian author and hunter Felix Salten.

Pixar Animation Studios

In 2006 the Walt Disney Company acquired Pixar Animation Studios, a leader in computer animation and innovative technical artistry. At the time, Pixar was known for its many successful hits such as Toy Story, The Incredibles, Finding Nemo, and Up. Pixar began in 1979 as the Graphics Group, part of the Computer Division of Lucasfilm before it was acquired by Apple Computer co-founder Steve Jobs in 1986. In 2013, Pixar released the blockbuster hit Frozen, which became the highest grossing animated feature ever.
